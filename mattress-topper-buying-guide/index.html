How to choose the right mattress topper

Updated June 2026Independently researchedNo paid placement.

Quick answer

A mattress topper can revive an uncomfortable bed without the cost of a new mattress. Focus on material (memory foam, latex, or down alternative), thickness (2–4 inches is typical), and your sleep position. For most people, a medium-density, 3-inch topper strikes the best balance of comfort and support.

What materials are available for mattress toppers?

The three main materials are memory foam, latex, and down or down-alternative fills. Memory foam conforms closely to your body and is excellent for pressure relief, but some versions sleep warm unless they are infused with gel or have an open-cell structure. Latex is springier, more responsive, and naturally breathable, making it a favorite for hot sleepers and those who want a bit of bounce. Down and down-alternative toppers are soft and fluffy, ideal for adding plushness to an already firm mattress, but they offer less support and may need frequent fluffing. Within each category, quality varies widely. A budget-friendly memory foam topper might use lower-density foam that breaks down quickly, while a premium latex topper can last a decade or more. Look for CertiPUR-US certification for foam and OEKO-TEX certification for any fabric covers, these ensure the materials meet basic safety and durability standards.

How thick should a mattress topper be?

Thickness directly affects the level of comfort and support. Most toppers range from 2 to 4 inches thick. A 2-inch topper provides a subtle change, enough to soften a firm mattress or add a bit of cushioning without altering the feel dramatically. A 3-inch topper is the most popular choice because it offers noticeable pressure relief without making you feel like you’re sinking too deeply. A 4-inch topper can feel like a completely new mattress surface, but it also raises the total height of your bed, which may affect how fitted sheets fit and how easy it is to get in and out of bed. Your body weight and preferred sleeping position should guide your choice. Lighter individuals and stomach sleepers usually do well with thinner toppers (2 inches). Heavier people and side sleepers often need the extra depth of a 3- or 4-inch model to properly cradle pressure points. If you share a bed, a medium thickness like 3 inches is a safe compromise for most couples.

Which mattress topper is best for back pain?

For lower back pain, the goal is to maintain a neutral spine while relieving pressure on the hips and shoulders. Memory foam toppers are often recommended because they contour closely and can reduce stiffness in the morning. However, not all memory foam is equal, a topper that is too soft will cause your hips to sink, exaggerating misalignment. Look for a medium-firm topper that is supportive enough to keep your spine in a straight line when you lie on your side or back. Latex toppers are another strong option because they provide a firmer, more even surface that still adapts to your body. They are especially good for people who have allergies or tend to sleep warm. If your back pain is related to an old, sagging mattress, a topper can help for a while, but it is not a permanent fix, a mattress that has lost its core support will eventually cause issues regardless of the topper you add.

How do I care for and clean a mattress topper?

Most mattress toppers come with a removable, machine-washable cover, which makes routine care simple. You should wash the cover every few months using a gentle cycle and cold water, then tumble dry on low heat. The topper itself should not be submerged in water, instead, spot-clean stains with a mild detergent and a damp cloth, then let it air dry completely to prevent mold or mildew. Latex toppers can be lightly vacuumed to remove dust and allergens. Memory foam toppers should never be folded or compressed for long periods, as this can damage the foam structure. A mattress protector placed over the topper will extend its life by shielding it from spills, sweat, and dust mites. Rotating the topper head-to-toe every six months helps it wear more evenly.

Memory foam vs. latex: which is better for you?

This decision comes down to your sleep style and temperature preference. Memory foam is unbeatable for deep contouring and motion isolation, if you share a bed with a restless partner, memory foam will keep most movement from transferring across the surface. The trade-off is that many memory foam toppers retain heat, though gel-infused and open-cell versions are significantly cooler than older designs. Latex is naturally breathable and resists dust mites and mold, making it a better choice for allergy sufferers and hot sleepers. It also has a quicker response time, you don’t feel stuck or slow to shift positions. The main downsides are cost (latex is almost always more expensive) and a firmer initial feel that some people find less luxurious. If you can, try a floor model or take advantage of a trial period to decide which sensation you prefer.

Frequently asked questions

How long does a mattress topper typically last?

A good-quality mattress topper should last between 3 and 5 years with proper care. Low-density foam toppers may show sagging sooner, while high-density memory foam or latex toppers can last 6 to 8 years. If you notice permanent indentations or loss of support, it is time to replace it.

Can I use a mattress topper on an air mattress?

Yes, but only with a flat, fully inflated air mattress. A topper can add cushioning to a firm air bed, but it cannot fix an underinflated or sagging mattress, the topper will just follow the contours of the uneven surface. Stick to a thin topper (2 inches) to avoid making the bed too tall or unstable.

Will a mattress topper fix a sagging mattress?

A topper can temporarily mask the feeling of a sagging mattress by adding a layer of cushioning on top, but it will not restore lost core support. If your mattress has deep body impressions or you wake up with back pain, the underlying mattress is likely worn out and should be replaced. A topper is best used to adjust the feel of a mattress that is still structurally sound.

Which topper is best for side sleepers?

Side sleepers generally need a softer topper to relieve pressure on the shoulders and hips. A 3- or 4-inch memory foam topper is a popular choice because it allows the heavier parts of the body to sink in while keeping the spine aligned. Latex toppers can work if you prefer a more responsive surface, but look for a soft or medium softness to avoid shoulder discomfort.

How do I stop my mattress topper from sliding around?

Many toppers come with corner straps, non-slip bottom layers, or a fitted sheet-style cover. If yours slides despite these features, a simple fix is to place a thin, non-slip rug pad or a mattress protector with a grippy underside between the mattress and the topper. Fitted sheets with deep pockets and elastic all around can also help hold everything in place.

In shortA mattress topper is one of the most affordable ways to improve your sleep, but it is not a one-size-fits-all fix. The best choice depends on your mattress condition, sleeping position, and comfort preferences. Take your time comparing materials and reading owner feedback, and always check the return policy so you have a way out if the feel doesn’t match your expectations.
